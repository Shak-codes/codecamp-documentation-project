Making tweets

All I'm going to say for advice on tweets is to think before you tweet. Very often I will see a tweet and simply wonder 'why was this made?' So I implore you all, before you tweet, think about how you would feel if somebody you very much disliked tweeted the same thing.

Examples of what not to tweet:

  • 'Quad on FTWin'
  • 'Like this tweet and I will...'
  • 'Ratio (unless you're Acid)'
  • 'We should play ___ mode only'
  • 'What if you could VOD review life'
  • 'I think I'm a better ___ than Kyo'
  • 'Any weapon can be viable'

Examples of what is okay to tweet:

  • Literally anything else

Notification Bell

You may not know, but Twitter has a notification bell for its users. A good friend of mine, @gamaroo typically responds to my tweets with 'noti gang.' For a while I was confused until I finally realized that Gamer is eluding to the fact that he is part of the 'Notification gang.' Those that turn on the notification bell for an individual will receive notifications when that user tweets. I suggest you join the noti gang for users you're particularly fond of, to support them as best you can.

Mute

The mute button is another extremely useful feature on Twitter. We spoke about following users that you like, however, sometimes you may find individuals that you aren't very fond of. In some cases, these people find their way onto your twitter feeds one way or another. Perhaps it's their terrible opinions or just the way they present themself that rubs you the wrong way. Luckily, the mute button ensures that you won't see tweets from those individuals at all. Very useful to keep your peace of mind as Twitter can become almost a warzone at times.

How to mute someone

Muting someone is simple! Just go to their page hit the three dots and select mute and voila! You'll never see these tweets again.

Block

Blocking someone is pretty similar to muting. This typically leads to the big question. 'Why choose one over the other?' Well there is a bit of a difference between blocking and muting. Blocking let's the targeted user know that they have been blocked, while muting does not provide the targeted individual any idea that they have been blocked.

An example where you may block someone.

report

When someone is just abnoxious, or plain rude, or harmful towards other individuals, you may have to resort to reporting them. Nobody likes someone that attacks others or puts them down and so the report button lets Twitter know exactly what that individual is doing so Twitter can take action. It's important to not abuse this button and only report individuals who are genuinely being problematic.
